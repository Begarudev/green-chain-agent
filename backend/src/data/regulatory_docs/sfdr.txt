Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation (SFDR) - Green Lending Requirements

The SFDR requires financial market participants to disclose how they integrate sustainability risks and consider adverse sustainability impacts in their investment and lending decisions.

ARTICLE 2 - DEFINITIONS

2.1 Sustainable Investment
An investment in an economic activity that:
- Contributes to an environmental objective (e.g., climate change mitigation)
- Does not significantly harm any environmental or social objective
- Follows good governance practices

2.2 Sustainability Risk
An environmental, social, or governance (ESG) event that could cause negative impact on the value of an investment or loan.

2.3 Principal Adverse Impact
Negative effects on sustainability factors resulting from investment or lending decisions.

ARTICLE 3 - TRANSPARENCY OF SUSTAINABILITY RISK POLICIES

Financial institutions must:
- Publish policies on integration of sustainability risks
- Describe how sustainability risks are managed
- Explain due diligence processes
- Disclose how sustainability risks affect returns

For green lending:
- Policies on agricultural loan sustainability assessment
- Risk management for environmental factors (drought, deforestation)
- Due diligence using satellite data and NDVI analysis
- Impact of sustainability scores on loan terms

ARTICLE 4 - TRANSPARENCY OF ADVERSE SUSTAINABILITY IMPACTS

Institutions must:
- Consider principal adverse impacts of investment/lending decisions
- Disclose how adverse impacts are identified and addressed
- Report on sustainability indicators
- Provide information on engagement with investees/borrowers

For agricultural loans:
- Monitor deforestation and land-use changes
- Track water usage and pollution risks
- Assess biodiversity impacts
- Engage with farmers on sustainable practices

ARTICLE 5 - TRANSPARENCY OF SUSTAINABILITY RISKS IN REMUNERATION

Remuneration policies must:
- Be consistent with integration of sustainability risks
- Not encourage excessive risk-taking
- Align with long-term sustainability objectives

ARTICLE 6 - PRE-CONTRACTUAL DISCLOSURES

Before entering into contracts, institutions must disclose:
- How sustainability risks are integrated
- Likely impacts of sustainability risks on returns
- Whether products consider principal adverse impacts
- Information on sustainability-related objectives

For loan products:
- How sustainability assessment affects loan approval
- Impact of sustainability scores on interest rates
- Monitoring and compliance requirements
- Environmental objectives of green lending programs

ARTICLE 7 - WEBSITE DISCLOSURES

Institutions must publish on their websites:
- Policies on sustainability risk integration
- Information on principal adverse impacts
- Description of due diligence processes
- Sustainability indicators and metrics

ARTICLE 8 - PERIODIC REPORTS

Regular reporting must include:
- Extent to which products meet sustainability objectives
- Assessment of sustainability impacts
- Progress toward sustainability targets
- Compliance with disclosed policies

For green lending portfolios:
- Percentage of loans meeting sustainability criteria
- Aggregate environmental impact (carbon, water, biodiversity)
- Default rates by sustainability score
- Compliance with LMA and EU Taxonomy standards

ARTICLE 9 - PRODUCT-LEVEL DISCLOSURES

9.1 Article 8 Products (Promoting Environmental/Social Characteristics)
- Clear description of environmental or social characteristics
- Information on how characteristics are met
- Details on sustainability indicators used
- Explanation of methodology

9.2 Article 9 Products (Sustainable Investment Objective)
- Description of sustainable investment objective
- Information on how objective is achieved
- Details on sustainability indicators
- Explanation of methodology and data sources

Green lending products typically fall under Article 8 or Article 9:
- Article 8: Loans that promote environmental characteristics (sustainable farming)
- Article 9: Loans with explicit sustainable investment objective (carbon reduction, biodiversity)

ARTICLE 10 - METHODOLOGY AND DATA SOURCES

Disclosures must include:
- Methodology for assessing sustainability
- Data sources and their reliability
- Limitations of data and methodology
- How data quality is ensured

For satellite-based assessment:
- NDVI data sources (Sentinel-2, AWS Open Data)
- Methodology for sustainability scoring
- Validation and verification processes
- Limitations and uncertainties

ARTICLE 11 - COMPLIANCE AND VERIFICATION

- Regular audits of sustainability claims
- Third-party verification where applicable
- Transparent reporting and disclosure
- Accountability for misstatements
