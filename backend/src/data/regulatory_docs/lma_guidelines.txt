LMA Green Lending Guidelines for Sustainable Agriculture

The Loan Market Association (LMA) promotes sustainable finance practices across Europe, the Middle East, and Africa. These guidelines outline best practices for green lending in agricultural sectors.

1. SUSTAINABILITY CRITERIA

1.1 Environmental Impact Assessment
- Loans for sustainable agriculture must demonstrate positive environmental impact
- Verification through satellite imagery, NDVI analysis, and on-ground assessments
- Minimum sustainability score of 60/100 for approval consideration
- Higher scores (70+) qualify for preferential interest rates

1.2 Sustainable Farming Practices
- Organic farming methods and reduced chemical usage
- Water conservation and efficient irrigation systems
- Biodiversity protection and habitat preservation
- Soil health maintenance and erosion prevention
- Crop rotation and sustainable land management

1.3 Climate Resilience
- Adaptation to climate change impacts
- Drought-resistant crop varieties
- Weather risk mitigation strategies
- Carbon sequestration potential

2. LOAN APPROVAL PROCESS

2.1 Documentation Requirements
- Farm location coordinates and boundary mapping
- Historical NDVI data (minimum 6 months)
- Deforestation risk assessment (2-year analysis)
- Weather and climate data analysis
- Sustainability score breakdown

2.2 Risk Assessment
- Vegetation health trends (improving/stable/declining)
- Deforestation detection (automatic rejection if detected)
- Weather risk factors (drought, frost, excessive rainfall)
- Consistency of farming practices over time

2.3 Loan Terms
- Interest rates: 4-8% based on sustainability score
- Maximum loan amount: $10,000 for micro-loans
- Repayment period: 12-24 months
- Monitoring: Quarterly NDVI checks for active loans

3. COMPLIANCE AND MONITORING

3.1 Ongoing Monitoring
- Automated monthly satellite checks for approved loans
- Alert system for declining sustainability scores
- Early warning for deforestation or land-use changes
- Compliance verification against original loan criteria

3.2 Non-Compliance Actions
- Warning notices for score declines
- Conditional requirements for improvement
- Loan restructuring for moderate issues
- Default procedures for severe violations

5. REGIONAL CONSIDERATIONS

5.1 EMEA Focus
- Adapt to regional agricultural practices
- Consider local climate patterns
- Respect cultural farming traditions
- Support smallholder farmers and rural communities

5.2 Market Standards
- Align with EU Taxonomy Regulation
- Comply with SFDR (Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation)
- Follow regional banking regulations
- Support industry-wide standardization
